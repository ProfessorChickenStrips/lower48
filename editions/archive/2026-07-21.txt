TASTE TUESDAY

Cúrate, Asheville. Katie Button's tapas bar has held the city's crown since 2011. The cooking is serious Spanish technique — imported Josper oven for the pulpo, a charcuterie program that flies whole jamóns from Extremadura, a gin-and-tonic cart with seventeen botanicals. The dining counter faces the open kitchen; reserve it if you can. Locals treat it like a second living room, which tells you everything. It books weeks out in summer, but they hold some bar seats for walk-ins if your Aug 15 plans stay loose. It's the meal that says "we're here" better than any other.

THE CULTURE FILE

Biltmore gave cinema one of its strangest footnotes: the 1994 thriller "Richie Rich" filmed there, casting Macaulay Culkin as the boy who lives in a 250-room mansion. The estate's Banquet Hall appears in "Hannibal" (2001) and "The Last of the Mohicans" (1992). But the best Asheville connection lives in music — Nina Simone was born twenty miles away in Tryon, and Thomas Wolfe wrote "Look Homeward, Angel" about his childhood here, a novel so scalding the town banned him for years. The art-deco Grove Arcade downtown opened in 1929 as "the most elegant building in America," shuttered during World War II as an Army office, and reopened in 2002 as the marketplace it was always meant to be.
