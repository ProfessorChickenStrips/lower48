**POSTCARD**

"There is a sumptuous variety about the New England weather that compels the stranger's admiration — and regret. The weather is always doing something there; always attending strictly to business; always getting up new designs and trying them on the people to see how they will go." — Mark Twain, who spent summers in New Hampshire and knew these states in all their moods.

**THE CULTURE FILE**

Asheville's been a haven for makers and mystics since the 1920s, when F. Scott Fitzgerald's wife Zelda spent time at Highland Hospital seeking treatment. Thomas Wolfe grew up here and immortalized it as "Altamont" in *Look Homeward, Angel* — his childhood home's now a museum on Spruce Street. The Blind Tiger itself occupies a 1903 building in the heart of what's become one of America's densest brewery districts: more breweries per capita than Portland or Denver. George Vanderbilt chose these mountains for Biltmore in 1889 specifically for the climate and the views — a quarter-billion-dollar bet (in today's money) that this was the most beautiful place in America to build a home.
